Straight answers

What changes—and what does not.

The platform is expanding, but the status of each surface remains explicit.

Is Rewards Pro still a Shopify app?

Yes. Shopify is the only production, self-serve commerce connector today and remains fully supported. The change is that Shopify becomes the first connector to a broader loyalty engine, rather than the permanent boundary of the product.

Can I install Rewards Pro on WooCommerce today?

Not yet. WooCommerce is the planned first full non-Shopify connector. “Planned” describes a chosen direction, not a released plugin or a delivery date.

Is there a public headless API now?

No standalone public platform API is being promised today. Its contracts are in design. We will call it available only when it has merchant authentication, versioned documentation, idempotency, signed webhooks, and a working reference integration.

Will every connector offer Shopify store credit?

No. Redemption capabilities differ by platform. Shopify can use native store credit; an early WooCommerce connector may use restricted single-use coupons. Rewards Pro will label the mechanism instead of pretending every platform has parity.

Are raffles, mystery boxes, and missions live for customers?

Their merchant configuration and core engines exist, but their Shopify customer-account participation routes are currently paused until session-token identity is securely bound to the correct shop and Rewards Pro customer. The page labels that state rather than presenting configuration as a live customer flow.

Will customers share one balance across stores?

A canonical identity and ledger make that direction possible, but cross-store and cross-platform balances are not available today. Identity linking, consent, currency, liability, and redemption rules must be designed first.

How do I influence which connector comes next?

Email contact@rewardspro.io with your platform, monthly order volume, and current reward flow. That context is more useful than a generic vote.
